Volta and the battery

Alessandro Volta invented the first electric battery in 1800, known as the voltaic pile, which could provide a continuous current into a circuit with alternating layers of zinc and silver or copper and pewter separated by cloth or cardboard soaked by brine (salt water) or NaOH.

The reliability of Volta’s invention brought about a great leap in electrical science and the creation of the scientific field of electrochemistry.

Niépce and photography

Though interested in the novel art of lithography, Niépce realized he lacked the skills for it. His use of camera obscura , a drawing aid that helped its users trace the “light paintings” projected onto their canvas, inspired him to look for a more effective way of capturing images. He was able to develop negative pictures as early as 1816, and from then developed a process dubbed heliography, or 'sun drawing', to create the first photographic image in 1827 on pewter.

Lister and antiseptic surgery

During his time, surgery was performed under unsanitary conditions due to a lack of knowledge regarding bacterial infections. Basing on Pasteur’s germ theory, Lister researched and developed antiseptic methods (handwashing and sterilization) aimed at improving hospital conditions.

Thurber and the typewriter

Charles Thurber was a firearm trader who was credited for patenting the first practical typewriter. Like others who attempted at creating a similar machine, Thurber aimed at aiding the blind.

However, the first typewriter created is thought to be Francesco Rampazetto’s 1575's scrittura tattile . The first patent for a machine similar to the typewriter was registered by Henry Mill in 1714.
